Chocolate Ice Cream Cake

Three-Layer Homemade Delight

For Each Layer:

  • Heavy cream - 2 cups per layer (6 cups total)
  • Dark chocolate - 200g per layer, chopped or melted (600g total)
  • Granulated sugar - ¬Ω cup per layer (1.5 cups total)
  • Vanilla extract - 1 tsp per layer (3 tsp total)
  • Salt - pinch per layer

Additional Materials:

  • Large mixing bowls
  • Electric mixer or whisk
  • 9-inch springform pan or cake mold
  • Plastic wrap
  • Freezer space

Layer 1 (Bottom Layer):

  1. Melt 200g dark chocolate in a double boiler or microwave until smooth
  2. In a large bowl, whip 2 cups heavy cream with ¬Ω cup sugar until soft peaks form
  3. Gradually fold in the melted chocolate and 1 tsp vanilla extract
  4. Pour into the bottom of your cake mold and freeze for 2-3 hours until firm

Layer 2 (Middle Layer):

  1. Repeat the chocolate melting process with another 200g chocolate
  2. Whip 2 cups cream with ¬Ω cup sugar until soft peaks form
  3. Fold in melted chocolate and vanilla, creating a slightly different consistency
  4. Gently spread over the first frozen layer and freeze for another 2-3 hours

Layer 3 (Top Layer):

  1. Melt the final 200g chocolate and let it cool slightly
  2. Whip remaining 2 cups cream with ¬Ω cup sugar until medium peaks form
  3. Fold in chocolate and vanilla for the final layer
  4. Spread evenly on top and freeze overnight (at least 6 hours)

Final Steps:

  • Unmolding: Run a warm knife around the edges before removing from the mold
  • Serving: Let sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes before slicing
  • Storage: Cover and store in freezer for up to 1 week
  • Decoration: Optional garnish with chocolate shavings or fresh berries

  • Use different chocolate percentages for each layer (70%, 60%, 50%) for varied flavors
