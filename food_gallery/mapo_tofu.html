Key Ingredients

Tofu: Silken or soft tofu, cut into cubes

Ground Pork: Adds richness and texture

Doubanjiang: Fermented chili bean paste - the soul of the dish

Sichuan Peppercorns: For that signature numbing sensation

Garlic & Ginger: Fresh aromatics

Green Onions: For freshness and color

Shaoxing Wine: Chinese cooking wine for depth

My Cooking Method

I start by carefully heating oil and blooming the Sichuan peppercorns to release their fragrant oils. The doubanjiang is then slowly cooked until fragrant, followed by the aromatics. The key is gentle handling of the tofu to keep it intact while absorbing all those beautiful flavors.

Personal Touch

My version includes extra garlic for depth and a generous garnish of fresh green onions. I also like to add a touch of dark soy sauce for color and use a cornstarch slurry to create the perfect glossy sauce that coats each piece of tofu.

Serving Suggestions

Best served hot over steamed jasmine rice with a side of simple stir-fried vegetables. The rice helps balance the heat while soaking up the delicious sauce. Perfect for sharing with family and friends!
