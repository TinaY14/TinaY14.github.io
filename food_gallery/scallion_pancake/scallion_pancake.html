Scallion pancakes are a beloved Chinese street food known for their flaky, crispy exterior and soft, layered interior packed with fresh scallions. This homemade version captures all the authentic flavors and textures that make this dish so irresistible as a snack or side dish.

For the Filling:

  • Fresh scallions - 1 large bunch, finely chopped
  • Salt - 1 teaspoon
  • Sesame oil - 1 tablespoon

For the Oil Paste:

  • All-purpose flour - 3 tablespoons
  • Vegetable oil - 3 tablespoons

For Cooking:

  • Vegetable oil for pan-frying

Step 1: Make the Dough

  1. Mix flour and salt in a large bowl
  2. Pour hot water into flour mixture, stirring with chopsticks
  3. Add cold water gradually until dough comes together
  4. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic
  5. Cover and rest for 30 minutes

Step 2: Prepare Oil Paste and Filling

  1. Heat 3 tablespoons oil in a small pan
  2. Add 3 tablespoons flour and ¬Ω teaspoon salt
  3. Cook until paste forms, then cool
  4. Mix chopped scallions with salt and sesame oil

Step 3: Roll and Layer

  1. Divide dough into 4-6 portions
  2. Roll each portion into a thin rectangle
  3. Spread oil paste evenly over surface
  4. Sprinkle scallion mixture generously
  5. Roll up tightly into a cylinder

Step 4: Shape the Pancakes

  1. Coil each cylinder into a spiral (like a snail shell)
  2. Tuck the end underneath
  3. Let rest for 10 minutes
  4. Gently roll each spiral into a flat pancake
  5. Be careful not to press too hard

Step 5: Cook to Golden Perfection

  1. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat
  2. Cook pancakes one at a time for 2-3 minutes per side
  3. Press gently with spatula to ensure even cooking
  4. Cook until golden brown and crispy
  5. Drain briefly on paper towels

üí° Secret to Perfect Pancakes

  • Hot Water Dough: Using hot water creates the tender, flaky texture
  • Resting Time: Don't skip the rest periods - they're crucial for workability
  • Oil Paste: This creates the layers and prevents the dough from sticking
  • Rolling Technique: Roll gently to maintain the layers you've created
  • Fresh Scallions: Use the white and light green parts for best flavor
  • Serving: Cut into wedges and serve hot for maximum crispiness
