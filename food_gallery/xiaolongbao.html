Delicate soup dumplings crafted with patience and skill

The Secret: Aspic Broth

Pork Stock: Made from pork bones, chicken, and aromatics

Gelatin Formation: Cooled stock becomes firm aspic

Filling Integration: Diced aspic mixed with seasoned ground pork

Steam Magic: Heat melts aspic back into delicious broth

Wrapper Technique

The wrapper (ÁöÆÂ≠ê) is crucial - it must be thin enough to be translucent yet strong enough to hold the soup. I roll each wrapper by hand, keeping the center slightly thicker than the edges for structural integrity. The pleating technique requires 18-20 folds for the authentic look.

Filling Preparation

Ground Pork: Fresh, with good fat content for juiciness

Seasonings: Soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, white pepper

Ginger: Fresh minced ginger for warmth

Aspic Cubes: The key to the soup inside

Steaming Process

I steam them in traditional bamboo baskets lined with cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. The steaming process takes exactly 8-10 minutes - too long and the wrappers break, too short and the aspic won't melt completely. The result is a delicate dumpling filled with hot, flavorful soup.

Eating Technique

The proper way is to carefully lift the xiaolongbao with chopsticks, place it on your spoon, make a small hole to release some steam, sip the soup, then enjoy the dumpling. It's an experience that combines taste, technique, and tradition.

Cultural Heritage

Originally from Shanghai, xiaolongbao represents the refined, subtle flavors of Jiangnan cuisine. Each dumpling is a work of art, showcasing the chef's skill and the rich culinary traditions of Eastern China.
