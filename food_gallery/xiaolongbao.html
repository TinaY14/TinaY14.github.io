Xiaolongbao are delicate soup dumplings that originated in Shanghai. Each dumpling contains a flavorful pork filling surrounded by rich, savory broth that's created during the steaming process. Making them from scratch requires patience and technique, but the result is incredibly rewarding.

Step 1: Make the Gelatin Stock (Day Before)

  1. Boil pork bones with ginger and green onion for 3-4 hours
  2. Strain and refrigerate overnight until it becomes jelly-like
  3. Cut the jellied stock into small cubes

Step 2: Prepare the Dough

  1. Mix flour and salt in a large bowl
  2. Gradually add warm water while mixing until dough forms
  3. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic
  4. Cover and rest for 30 minutes

Step 3: Make the Filling

  1. Mix ground pork with all seasonings
  2. Stir in one direction for 5 minutes until sticky
  3. Gently fold in the jellied stock cubes
  4. Refrigerate filling until ready to use

Step 4: Assemble the Dumplings

  1. Roll dough into a long cylinder and cut into small pieces
  2. Roll each piece into thin circles (thinner edges, thicker center)
  3. Place 1 tablespoon filling in center
  4. Pleat the edges (18-20 pleats) and twist to seal at top
  5. Place on parchment paper squares

Step 5: Steam to Perfection

  1. Arrange dumplings in steamer, leaving space between each
  2. Steam over high heat for 12-15 minutes
  3. Turn off heat and let sit for 2 minutes before opening
  4. Serve immediately while hot

ğŸ’¡ Expert Tips

  • Pleating Technique: Practice makes perfect - aim for 18-20 pleats for authentic look
  • Soup Secret: The gelatin stock melts during steaming to create the soup inside
  • Eating Method: Bite a small hole first, sip the broth, then eat the dumpling
  • Wrapper Thickness: Keep edges thin but center thick to prevent tearing
  • Timing: Serve immediately - the soup cools quickly and loses its magic
