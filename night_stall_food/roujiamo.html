Roujiamo is often called the "Chinese hamburger" - a popular street food from Shaanxi province featuring tender, slow-cooked meat stuffed inside a crispy flatbread. It's one of the oldest forms of sandwich in the world!

The Bread (Mo):

  • Wheat flour flatbread

  • Crispy outside, soft inside
  • Split open to hold filling

The Filling (Rou):

  • Slow-braised pork (most common)
  • Or beef/lamb in Muslim areas
  • Cooked with 20+ spices
  • Tender and flavorful
  • Chopped finely

Braising Spices:

  • Star anise
  • Cinnamon
  • Bay leaves
  • Sichuan peppercorns
  • Ginger
  • Soy sauce
  • Rock sugar

üí° Cultural Notes

Roujiamo has over 2,000 years of history! The meat is braised for hours in a complex blend of spices until incredibly tender. The bread is baked fresh in traditional clay ovens. It's a perfect street food - portable, delicious, and filling. The Shaanxi province version with pork is most famous, but Muslim-style lamb roujiamo is equally beloved.
