Tips: Takoyaki is a snack from Japan. It's a delicious food which you should definitely have a try. It's really common in the Chinese Night Stall market. It has a unique sauce on each ball and will make you feel satisfied with the octopus on every single bite!

Takoyaki originated in Osaka, Japan in the 1930s and has become one of Japan's most iconic street foods. The name literally means "grilled octopus" and these spherical balls are cooked in a special takoyaki pan with half-spherical molds. The secret to perfect takoyaki is the crispy exterior and creamy, almost liquid interior.

Cooking Tips: The batter should be quite liquid, and the balls are turned frequently while cooking to achieve the perfect round shape. The traditional toppings include takoyaki sauce (similar to Worcestershire sauce), Japanese mayonnaise, aonori (dried seaweed flakes), and katsuobushi (bonito flakes) that dance from the heat. Best enjoyed piping hot - be careful not to burn your tongue!
