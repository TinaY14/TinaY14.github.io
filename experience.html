Chinese Night Stall Food Collection

Chuanr (串儿, Skewers)

Chuanr are grilled meat skewers, often lamb or chicken, seasoned with cumin, chili, and other spices. They are a staple of night markets across China and are enjoyed for their smoky, spicy flavor.

Shengjianbao (生煎包, Pan-fried Pork Buns)

Shengjianbao are juicy pork buns with a crispy bottom, pan-fried to perfection and sprinkled with sesame and scallions. The filling is savory and the dough is soft yet crunchy on the base.

Jianbing (煎饼, Chinese Crepe)

Jianbing is a popular breakfast street food, made from a thin crepe filled with egg, scallions, cilantro, crispy wonton, and savory sauces. It is folded and served hot, perfect for eating on the go.

Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐)

Stinky tofu is a fermented tofu snack with a strong aroma and a crispy exterior. It is usually deep-fried and served with chili sauce and pickled vegetables, loved by adventurous eaters.

Roujiamo (肉夹馍, Chinese Hamburger)

Roujiamo is a sandwich of slow-cooked, spiced pork stuffed in a crispy flatbread. It is often called the Chinese hamburger and is a filling, flavorful street food option.

Douhua (豆花, Tofu Pudding)

Douhua is a soft tofu dessert served with sweet syrup or savory toppings. It is silky, smooth, and refreshing, making it a popular treat at night markets.
