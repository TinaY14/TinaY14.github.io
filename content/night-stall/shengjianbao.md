# â„¹ï¸ About Shengjianbao

Shengjianbao are pan-fried pork buns, a beloved Shanghai breakfast and snack. Unlike steamed buns, these are pan-fried to create a crispy, golden bottom while the top stays soft and fluffy. They're filled with juicy pork and rich broth.

# ğŸ¥„ Components

**The Dough:**
- Semi-leavened wheat flour dough
- Soft and fluffy on top
- Crispy and golden on bottom

**The Filling:**
- Ground pork
- Pork gelatin (creates soup)
- Ginger
- Green onions
- Soy sauce
- Sesame oil
- Sugar

**Toppings:**
- Sesame seeds
- Chopped scallions

# ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ³ Cooking Method

1. Fill dough with pork mixture
2. Pleat and seal at the top
3. Place pleated-side up in hot oiled pan
4. Pan-fry until bottom is crispy
5. Add water and cover to steam top
6. Uncover to crisp bottom again
7. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and scallions

# ğŸ’¡ Eating Tips

**Proper Technique:**
1. Let cool slightly (the soup inside is very hot!)
2. Bite a small hole in the side
3. Sip the hot, flavorful broth
4. Eat the bun

The magic is in the gelatin that melts during cooking to create soup inside! Best enjoyed fresh from the pan at a Shanghai street stall. The crispy bottom contrasting with soft top and juicy filling is perfection!
