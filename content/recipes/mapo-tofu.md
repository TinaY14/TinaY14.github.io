Mapo Tofu is one of the most famous dishes from Sichuan cuisine, known for its "m√° l√†" (numbing and spicy) flavor profile. This homemade version features silky tofu, savory ground pork, and a rich chili bean sauce that creates the perfect balance of heat and umami.

# ü•ò Ingredients

**Main Ingredients:**

- Silky tofu (soft or medium-firm) - 1 block (400g)
- Ground pork - 150g
- Green onions - 3-4 stalks, chopped

**Sauce & Seasonings:**

- Doubanjiang (chili bean paste) - 2 tablespoons
- Garlic - 4 cloves, minced
- Ginger - 1 inch piece, minced
- Sichuan peppercorns - 1 teaspoon, ground
- Light soy sauce - 2 tablespoons
- Dark soy sauce - 1 teaspoon
- Chicken stock - 1 cup
- Cornstarch - 1 tablespoon mixed with 2 tbsp water
- Cooking oil - 3 tablespoons
- Salt and sugar to taste

# üë©‚Äçüç≥ Cooking Instructions

**Step 1: Prepare the Tofu**

1. Cut tofu into 2cm cubes, being gentle to maintain shape
2. Bring water to boil, add 1 tsp salt, and blanch tofu for 2 minutes
3. Drain carefully and set aside (this removes excess water and firms up the tofu)

**Step 2: Cook the Pork**

1. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in wok over medium-high heat
2. Add ground pork and cook until browned and crispy, breaking it up as it cooks
3. Push pork to one side of the wok

**Step 3: Build the Sauce**

1. Add remaining oil to the empty side of wok
2. Add minced garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant
3. Add doubanjiang (chili bean paste) and cook for 1 minute until oil turns red
4. Mix everything together in the wok

**Step 4: Combine and Simmer**

1. Pour in chicken stock, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce
2. Bring to a gentle boil, then carefully add the blanched tofu
3. Simmer for 3-4 minutes, gently stirring occasionally
4. Add cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce
5. Sprinkle ground Sichuan peppercorns and adjust seasoning

**Step 5: Final Touches**

1. Transfer to serving dish
2. Garnish generously with chopped green onions
3. Serve immediately with steamed rice

# üí° Pro Tips

- **Tofu Choice:** Use silky or soft tofu for authentic texture, but handle gently
- **Authentic Heat:** Good doubanjiang is key - look for Pixian brand for best flavor
- **Sichuan Peppercorns:** Toast them lightly before grinding for maximum numbing effect
- **Extra Garlic:** My version uses extra garlic for deeper flavor
- **Serving:** Best enjoyed hot with plenty of steamed white rice
