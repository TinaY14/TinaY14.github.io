Egg waffles (Gai Daan Jai) are a popular Hong Kong street snack featuring a golden, crispy exterior with soft, cake-like bubbles. The distinctive bubble pattern makes them instantly recognizable and fun to eat.

# üí° Cultural Notes

Egg waffles originated in 1950s Hong Kong and have become an iconic street food. The special griddle creates the signature bubble shape. Best eaten fresh and hot - the contrast between crispy exterior and soft interior is the key! Now popular worldwide, especially in Asian dessert shops.
